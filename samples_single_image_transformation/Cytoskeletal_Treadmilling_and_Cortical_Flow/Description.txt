Treat the image as a cellular cortex where channels represent an actin-like structural payload and a signaling gradient dictating direction. The transformation continuously adds data at the leading edge of features and removes it from the trailing edge, causing the texture to treadmill. Unlike simple directional blurring, treadmilling conserves the structural integrity of pixel groupings while moving them. This tests vision by introducing directional, persistent streaming of textures that preserve structural integrity while translating them, evaluating how well visual models track coherent motion and recognize shapes under continuous, anisotropic self-deformation. It can be implemented by using a gradient channel to define a vector field of plus/minus ends, then shifting pixel intensity along this field while strictly maintaining mass conservation—adding intensity at advancing fronts and subtracting it at receding ends to simulate polymerization and depolymerization.
