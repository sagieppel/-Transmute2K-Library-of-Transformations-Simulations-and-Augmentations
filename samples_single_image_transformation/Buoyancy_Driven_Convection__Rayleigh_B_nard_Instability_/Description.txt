Treat the image channels as layers of a fluid with differing densities and temperatures. By applying a virtual heat source, thermal expansion is simulated. Lighter, hotter pixel values become buoyant and rise, while heavier, cooler pixels sink. This forms Rayleigh-Bénard convection cells—rolling, emergent vortices where channels churn and mix. It visually warps the image into structured, hexagonal cells of upwelling and downwelling colors, giving a dynamic, liquid-marble aesthetic simulating material layers erupting through each other.
