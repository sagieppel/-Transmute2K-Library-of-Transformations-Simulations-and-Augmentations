Concept: Based on the thermodynamics of creating silica aerogel. Image A acts as a wet, saturated gel block. Instead of typical evaporation, the transition mimics supercritical drying where the liquid inside transitions directly into a gas without destroying the delicate micro-structure. Visually, the dense liquid boils into a hazy, highly refractive distortion, leaving behind a structurally intact but ghostly, hyper-lightweight 'blueprint' of Image B that slowly condenses back into a fully opaque state. Vision testing: This targets the extraction of volumetric and refractive data by the human eye, challenging edge-detection and depth perception through radical shifts in Index of Refraction (IOR) and volumetric transparency. Implementation: Utilize a volumetric bounding simulation where a high-frequency, low-amplitude jitter mask mimics the critical boundary phase. Over time, the refraction mask of Image A translates into pure transmission, while the albedo of Image B is interpolated inside an additive volumetric shader that gradually normalizes into standard opaque diffuse scattering.
