This transition models the penetration of an external magnetic field into a Type-II superconductor. Image A represents the perfectly diamagnetic superconducting state, repelling change. As transitioning magnetic energy (Image B) increases, it cannot seep in smoothly; it must penetrate as discrete, quantized magnetic vortices (Abrikosov vortices). The depth map and roughness map of Image A serve as the 'pinning landscape' (crystalline defects). As vortices of Image B enter, they are physically dragged and snapped into the deepest crevices and highly roughened areas of Image A, mapping their topology. Once the local density of pinned vortices exceeds the critical current threshold, the superconducting state collapses, cascading outward and snapping the rest of the localized region into Image B. This tests dynamic algorithmic routing based on topological defect mapping, localized critical thresholding, and continuous vs. discrete (quantum) spatial propagation.
